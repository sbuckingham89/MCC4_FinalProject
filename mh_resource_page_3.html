Support Student



Mental Health Accessibility



And Reduce Hardship

Learn how you can help & get involved as a(n):

Get involved as a student:

Volunteer as a peer counselor or trained supportive listener.

Join or start an Active Minds or National Alliance on Mental Health chapter on your campus.

Organize a free student mental wellness event.

Remove stigma by sharing your own mental health journey story.

Connect potential donors to a campus Mental Wellness Journey Sponsorship program or give yourself.

Get involved in other related campus programs that address student hardships and stressors, such as food or housing insecurity.

How you can help as a Campus Faculty member:

Stay informed about campus resources for students that need free or low-cost mental health support, and become a resource for students.

Learn more about identifying struggling students and how to help from our Campus Counseling Center.

Get involved as a mentor or start a chapter for programs focused on accessible mental health or advocacy.

Alumni, Donor, and Community Member opportunities to get involved:

Donate and sponsor a student that is experiencing financial hardship to access basic mental health care.

Donate or sponsor other campus programs to meet student basic needs and reduce stress caused by hardship.

Organize a free or sponsored student mental wellness event.

For licensed mental health professionals that want to help:

Join our list of therapists & counselors that volunteer to offer some free or special sliding-scale student rates.

Volunteer to train or talk to a campus peer counselor program group.

Work with our Campus Counseling Center to offer a free virtual support group or mental wellness event to students.
